# The naties are a six-century Lindy institution

The *naties* are *zes eeuwen* institutions, and the persistence is a clean demonstration for the Lindy/antifragility register. Suppressed as guilds under the French Revolution, *reinstated by Napoleon*, then metabolized into modern cooperatives and firms — Katoen Natie (incorporated 1854, still operating), Tabaknatie, Wijngaard Natie, and the Hessenatie itself, which merged with Noord Natie in 2002 into Hesse-Noord Natie under Singapore's PSA.

A corporate shell that survives the *abolition of its own legal category*, changes of régime, and full industrialization while its contents — goods, technology, ownership form — turn over completely: that is institutional persistence of the shell independent of its contents, which is exactly what the Lindy claim needs. The unit of survival is the partitioned franchise, not the men or the cargo passing through it.
