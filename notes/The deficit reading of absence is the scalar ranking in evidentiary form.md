# The deficit reading of absence is the scalar ranking in evidentiary form

Harris and Kuran commit the same methodological error, one level below the ranking of forms: both read institutional absence as a **lack**. For Harris the missing corporation is missing enabling building blocks — absence as incapacity. For Kuran the missing accumulation is the wrong inheritance and partnership rules — absence as brake. In each case the absence is a hole where something ought to have been, and the account's task is to explain the hole.

Negative selection reverses the sign. The absence is not a hole but a **mark** — the trace an active filter leaves when it declines an available form. Absence stops being explanandum-as-deficiency and becomes explanans: evidence about the criterion. See [[Refusals are observations of the filter not inferences from survivors]].

The diagnosis that unifies the critique: the deficit reading of absence *is* the scalar ranking in evidentiary form. "Absent relative to Europe" smuggles the benchmark back in through the evidentiary door — which is why Harris must hedge, and why Kuran reads as a brake narrative however carefully he guards the value judgment. Negative selection is benchmark-free: it describes the filter in its own terms, by what it admits and what it refuses, with no European yardstick anywhere in the inference. So the refusal method is the opening anti-scalar move applied one level down — to the reading of evidence rather than to the ranking of forms. The trade-off surface and the negative-selection reading are the same commitment seen at two depths.
