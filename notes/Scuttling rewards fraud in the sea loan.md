# Scuttling rewards fraud in the sea loan

The faked loss is the borrower-side mirror of the lender's decoupling — mirror in *direction*, utterly different in *kind*. The lender games the *distribution*: he rearranges his exposure ex ante and honestly, and still bears the systematic tail — a *disfavored contract*, not a crime. The merchant games the *realization*: he falsifies which state obtained, ex post, by lying. Compactly: the lender deceives *himself* (the ensemble-average illusion), the merchant deceives *the other party*. Self-deception versus fraud; to call them equivalent flattens the line between a bad contract and a lie.

Archetype: Hegestratos and Zenothemis in Demosthenes, *Against Zenothemis* (Or. 32) — scuttling the vessel to claim the loss and pocket loan *and* cargo, among the oldest recorded financial frauds. Islam judges the faked loss not under the risk-sharing rubric but under **fraud / breach of trust**: *ghishsh* (deception — *man ghashshanā fa-laysa minnā*), *akl amwāl al-nās bi'l-bāṭil* (Q 2:188; Q 4:29), and *khiyāna* where capital was held on trust. Because held on *amāna* rather than seized from a guarded place (*ḥirz*), classical fiqh treats it not as *ḥadd* theft (*sariqa*) but as *taʿzīr*, discretionary penalty (**verify ḥadd/taʿzīr hadith wording against fiqh sources**).

The structural point: the sea loan pins payoff to a single, gameable, hard-to-verify event (arrival vs loss) and does worse than permit the fraud — it *rewards* it. Fake the sinking and keep cargo *and* principal; the forgiveness-on-loss feature puts a bounty on manufacturing the loss. The *qirad* gives no such bounty. So **gharar** was never only about metaphysical uncertainty; it was a *verification-and-moral-hazard* objection — doctrine disfavors a contract whose payoff turns on an unverifiable event *because it pays people to lie about the state of the world*. Comparative kicker: Athens met the fraud *ex post* by litigation (Demosthenes in court); Islamic law met it *ex ante* by contract design (favoring the form in which faking gains nothing).
