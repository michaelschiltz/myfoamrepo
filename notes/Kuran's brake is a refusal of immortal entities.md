# Kuran's brake is a refusal of immortal entities

Kuran's *The Long Divergence* (2011) rests on two mechanisms: the non-perpetuity of the Islamic partnership, which dissolved on the death of any partner, and Qurʾanic inheritance (*farāʾiḍ*), which fragmented estates among many heirs. Together they prevented capital accumulation and durable enterprise. Harris takes him up in chapter 12 [verify].

The reply is the anti-scalar move already deployed against Harris. Kuran reads dissolution-on-death and inheritance fragmentation as a **brake** — a single ranking on which the corporation is the higher rung and the Islamic settlement the arrested one. Recast the same facts on a trade-off surface and they read differently: those rules refuse the accumulation of immortal, decoupled entities. They preserve outcome-coupling and decline the veil. Not backwardness — a different point on the surface. See [[Lock-in and outcome-coupling are distinct bindings]].

The general principle: the Eurocentrism charge only bites where a scalar ranking is presupposed. Remove the ranking and there is no superior form left to be Eurocentric *about*. This is why the trade-off formulation is analytically cleaner than Harris's defensive apparatus — it dissolves the anxiety instead of managing it.

Guard the inverse error. The claim is *not* that the Islamic settlement was better, only that it optimized a different variable at a stated cost. See [[Guard against reverse-teleology]].
