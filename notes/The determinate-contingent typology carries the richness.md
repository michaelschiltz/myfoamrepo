# The determinate-contingent typology carries the richness

In WP1 the *richness* of premodern financial thinking — the demonstration that these are coherent alternatives, not primitiveness — is carried not by the transmission guardrail but by two exhibits that should be promoted ahead of the philology.

First, the **determinate / contingent typology**: how different traditions classify obligations by whether the governing event is fixed or aleatory — *gharar* in Islamic doctrine, *qingzhong* (輕重) in the Chinese apparatus, and Japanese practice. This typology is what shows the traditions answering a *different default question* coherently, rather than failing to reach ours.

Second, the **general-average finding**: the maritime loss-sharing rule has *no overland analogue*, for structural and material reasons (the ship is the indivisible joint venture; the caravan is not). That absence is itself evidence — it shows the pooling forms are shaped by the physics of the venture, not diffused as a template. Both belong where the reader was promised the payoff, ahead of the Sogdian/Sasanian genealogy.
