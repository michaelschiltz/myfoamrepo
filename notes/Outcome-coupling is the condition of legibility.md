# Outcome-coupling is the condition of legibility

Outcome-coupling carries an epistemic function beneath its normative one, but the argument has to be built correctly or it collapses on first inspection.

Clear away the false version first. Non-scalability does **not** enforce learning by leaving untenable organizations to swift local extinction. If the *qirad* dissolves at the venture's end and on the death of either party *regardless of performance*, then dissolution carries no fitness information at all: death that is automatic is selectively neutral at the level of the entity. Dissolution-by-design destroys the very differential on which selection operates. One cannot have selection over organizations engineered to die on schedule.

The information-bearing event is the **settlement**, not the dissolution. Because the *qirad* is outcome-coupled, every venture terminates in an unambiguous, jointly observed reckoning — profit divided by the agreed ratio, or capital loss borne by the *rabb al-māl*. That reckoning is a truthful signal, emitted at the frequency of venture completion and read by the mechanism that actually selects: funding and entrustment. The agent who returns losses stops being entrusted; the practice that settles badly stops being copied. The unit of selection is therefore the *practice*, culturally transmitted with differential adoption — not the firm. Stating it this way also avoids the group-selection quarrel, which this argument does not need.

The general claim: **coupling is what makes performance legible.** A stipulated premium, a diversified portfolio return, and a share price all blur the signal, because performance cannot be read off a return fixed in advance or off an aggregate. Legibility is thus a second, epistemic justification for *al-ghunm bi-l-ghurm*, sitting beneath the justice one.
