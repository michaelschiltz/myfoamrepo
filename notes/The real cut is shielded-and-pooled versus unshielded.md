# The real cut is shielded-and-pooled versus unshielded

Drop the West / non-West axis as the primary contrast — it is both historically false and Orientalism-bait. Western Europe before roughly 1600 also had to respect the time average, because it too could not offload the absorbing barrier. The real cut is **shielded-and-pooled versus unshielded**, and it runs *inside* every civilization.

Tokugawa Japan engineered its own barrier-management: *mujin* and *tanomoshi-kō* rotating credit, village-level mutual liability, and above all the *ie* as an entity that outlives its individual members — an entity-shielding device operating on the lineage rather than the firm (see [[The ie as entity-shielding]]). So the comparative claim is not "Japan optimized the time average, the West the ensemble." It is that multiple civilizations built different barrier-management institutions, and only *one* lineage went on to produce a *semantic apparatus* — the discipline of economics — that universalized the ensemble average and exported it as the definition of rationality.
