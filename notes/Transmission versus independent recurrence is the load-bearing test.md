# Transmission versus independent recurrence is the load-bearing test

The comparative claim carries one structural vulnerability: the functional equivalence documented across traditions might be **transmission** — a single template diffused along the trade routes — rather than **independent recurrence**. If the pooling forms are all one thing that travelled, the convergence evidence collapses into a diffusion story and the "multiple civilizations independently arrived at barrier-management" thesis fails.

Central Eurasia is where the objection is confronted, since the Silk Road is the one corridor along which the East Asian and Islamic-Mediterranean strands demonstrably met. The discriminating move is not to deny transmission but to *document* a clean case of it, so that its signature can be told apart from independent origination elsewhere. The heaviest evidentiary load therefore falls exactly where the objection lives.
