# The qirad envelops the sea loan

The *qirad* (*mudaraba*; Latin *commenda*) is a profit-and-loss-sharing partnership: its return is a *share of realized profit*, not a stipulated premium, and it is contingent on *commercial outcome*, not merely physical arrival. It therefore *envelops* the sea loan's function on cleaner doctrinal terms — it absorbs the sea loan's peril-coverage *inside* a broader loss-sharing (if the goods are lost the venture yields no profit and the investor bears the loss anyway) while pricing commercial risk *without* a stipulated premium.

This is why the sea loan did not migrate into Islamic law: **substitution, not blockage.** The *qirad* already held the niche — same risky voyages, same sea-peril tail — and held it immaculately. The doctrine disfavors the sea loan; a clean indigenous substitute pre-empts it; therefore no pressure to build a workaround.

And it convicts Harris on his own terms. The *qirad* is *his* prime migratory institution — the one he tracks out of Islamic Arabia as the commenda. He holds both instruments, but treats the sea loan's non-migration and the *qirad*'s migration as *separate facts* rather than two faces of one competitive-substitution dynamic: the *qirad* wins the home niche, so the sea loan has nowhere to land.
