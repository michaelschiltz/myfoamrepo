# Natie is a false cognate

The overlap between the Cádiz *nación flamenca* and the Antwerp *natie* runs through the *word* and the *corporate genus*, not the function — and the shared word is a decoy. Both descend from Middle Dutch *nacie* (< French *nation*, "group of common descent"), but in the Southern Low Countries the term specialized as the Castilian never did: by the late Middle Ages *natie* in Antwerp simply *meant* "corporation/guild" (so glossed in the *Woordenboek der Nederlandsche Taal*). Two branches off one stem, diverging on membership logic:

- the merchant *natio* — a corporate community of *foreigners* (the Cádiz *flamenca*);
- the Antwerp harbour *natie* — a monopolistic *labour / cargo-handling* corporation of *natives* (earliest attestation a 1460 Haringvliet dispute, dockworkers banding into *nacien* to claim the *alleenrecht*).

Same word, same corporate genus, opposite membership logic. The specific trap: the **Hessenatie** is *not* a nation of Hessians the way the *flamenca* is a nation of Flemings — "Hessen" names the *goods and route* (the *Hessenwagens* down the *Hessenwegen* from Augsburg). Pair them on the strength of the word and a referee catches you conflating an ethno-jurisdictional merchant community with a functional cargo cartel.
