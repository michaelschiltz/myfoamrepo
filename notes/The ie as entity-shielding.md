# The ie as entity-shielding

The Japanese *ie* (家) — the household as an entity that outlives its individual members — is an entity-shielding device operating on the *lineage* rather than the firm. It partitions continuity from any particular member's mortality or ruin, so the barrier that would absorb an individual does not absorb the house. It is the Japanese anchor for the claim that barrier-management institutions arose *inside* many civilizations (see [[The real cut is shielded-and-pooled versus unshielded]]), and it belongs to the perpetual-monopoly / perpetual-entity facet alongside the *naties* and the *kabu-nakama*.
