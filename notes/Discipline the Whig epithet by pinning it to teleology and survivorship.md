# Discipline the Whig epithet by pinning it to teleology and survivorship

"Whiggish" has become a term of abuse, and a sharp referee will read it as a rhetorical shortcut unless it is disciplined. Cite Butterfield and specify *which* of the four vices you are charging: pin it to **teleology-plus-survivorship-bias**, and explicitly *disavow* the cheaper "judging the past by present standards" version.

The disavowal is not optional, because you *are* applying a present theoretical apparatus (ergodicity economics) to the past, and you do not want to hand the objector an equivocation. The charge is not that past actors failed to know what we know; it is that the *historian's* narrative smuggles the outcome into the account of the process. Keep the indictment on the narration, not on the actors.
