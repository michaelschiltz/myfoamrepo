# Split provenance into priority and independence

"Provenance" names two questions the philological literature runs together, and only one is digressive.

**Origin-priority** — who arrived first, whether the *qirad* predates the *commenda*, whether the fountainhead is Sasanian or Arab. This is genuinely digressive for EE, and it is where the nationalist and civilizational-chauvinist energy collects. From a time-average/selection standpoint paternity contributes nothing: what makes a cooperative form interesting is that it survives and travels across material contexts — a claim about robustness, not about who holds the patent. Demoting priority is therefore not evasion but the correct level of analysis, and it inoculates against the nationalism as a by-product. Foreground the demotion openly.

**Transmission versus independent recurrence** — whether the instances are causally independent or one lineage that spread. This is **not** digressive; it is load-bearing, by Galton's Problem. Persistence across contexts is evidence of robustness *only if the instances are independent*. If they descend from one source by contact, "persistence across many societies" collapses into "one thing that travelled," and the effective n is one. The robustness inference the project leads with is epistemically parasitic on this question. See [[Transmission versus independent recurrence is the load-bearing test]].

So the instruction is not "background provenance" but **split it**: retire origin-priority to the footnotes on explicit EE grounds, and keep the independence test in the foreground because it guarantees the robustness inference. The two look like one question only because the literature conflates them. Backgrounding provenance wholesale would saw off the branch Objective 1 and the WP1 architecture sit on.
