# Identity-wrapper and perpetual-monopoly are distinct shielding facets

The sharpest thing about the *nación flamenca* — the *identity-wrapper / flag-of-convenience* argument, where the corporate form masks a foreign principal and arbitrages access — does **not** transfer to the *naties*. There is no naturalization, no excluded principal, no access arbitrage; the porters were not hiding a foreign identity.

So the two illuminate *different* facets of the corporate taxonomy, and conflating them makes the argument look sloppier than it is:

- the **identity-wrapper** facet (the *flamenca*): the shell shields a *who* — a masked principal — enabling access otherwise denied;
- the **perpetual asset-partitioned monopoly** facet (the *naties*): the shell shields a *what* — an operating franchise held as property — enabling persistence.

Treat them as two distinct sub-mechanisms under the one corporate umbrella of [[Entity-shielding]], and say so explicitly.
