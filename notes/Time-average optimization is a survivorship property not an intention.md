# Time-average optimization is a survivorship property not an intention

Do not impute time-average optimization to premodern actors as a *conscious objective* — they had no such concept, and a referee will destroy any sentence that implies they did. Make the claim **dynamical and selective**, not intentional: the institutions and behaviours that persisted were those whose *realized* trajectories tracked time-average growth, because the alternatives hit the absorbing barrier and were removed from the sample.

This is the Lindy / skin-in-the-game register doing legitimate work. Time-average "optimization" is a *survivorship property*, not a chosen program. Ensemble-average optimization only becomes *sustainable* once shielding institutions absorb the barrier on the agent's behalf. Framed this way, the emergence of economics is not a shift in what societies were maximizing; it is the moment a society built enough barrier-absorption to make ensemble reasoning survivable, and then wrote that local condition down as universal theory.

Open question to resolve before the strong causal claim: is the ensemble/time distinction the *cause* of the conceptual apparatus, or the retrospective *diagnostic* we are now entitled to apply? The defensible version is the latter plus a selection mechanism; the strong version owes a concrete transmission story (a specific instrument — VOC share, tontine, marine underwriting — licensing a specific concept).
