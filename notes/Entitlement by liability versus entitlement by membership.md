# Entitlement by liability versus entitlement by membership

Two theories of what entitles a party to a return, and the cut between them is the crown of the comparison.

**Entitlement by liability.** Return is *constituted by* the bearing of loss. *Al-ghunm bi-l-ghurm* — gain accompanies liability. *Al-kharāj bi-l-ḍamān* — the yield belongs to him who bears the risk of loss [verify hadith attribution and the standard *qāʿida* formulations]. On this theory a return decoupled from liability is not merely imprudent or fraud-prone; it is **not yours**. That is a doctrine of title and desert, not of incentive design, and it is much stronger than the moral-hazard reading that survives translation into the modern idiom.

**Entitlement by membership.** To obtain an entity that outlives its members and holds capital nobody may withdraw, a legal person must be interposed between the natural persons and the venture. Once interposed, entitlement no longer flows from bearing loss; it flows from *holding a share in the entity*. The share becomes the title.

That substitution is the exact content of the claim that the corporation buys duration and scale by paying in coupling. Restated precisely: *al-ghunm bi-l-ghurm* is the proposition the corporate form repeals.
