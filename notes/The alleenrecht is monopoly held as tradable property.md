# The alleenrecht is monopoly held as tradable property

The deep parallel between the *naties* and the corporate forms of the project is structural, and on one axis the *naties* are a *stronger* exemplar than the Cádiz *cofradía*. The natie's *alleenrecht* — its exclusive right to handle a defined class of goods at its berths — was held as *property* and traded in *shares*: the *natiebazen* were literally *aandeelhouders*, each fronting a crew of *natiegasten*.

That is **asset-partitioning around a productive franchise**, not a devotional corpus. It answers the standing objection to the confraternity as an entity-shielding exemplar — that its shielded assets are only chapel-and-relief, not commercial. Here the entity holds an *operating monopoly* as its shielded corpus, much closer to the firm-assets version of [[Entity-shielding]]. The monopoly right is the property; the property is partitioned; the partition is what shields.
