# Lock-in and outcome-coupling are distinct bindings

Two bindings must be held apart, and running them together wrecks the argument.

**Temporal binding** — capital lock-in, non-withdrawability, perpetual succession. The claim cannot be liquidated at will and the entity survives its members.

**Outcome-coupling** — no party may draw a return fixed independently of the realized result. Payoff tracks the venture's actual outcome.

Islamic doctrine insists on the second and declines the first. *Riba* and *gharar* police outcome-coupling; nothing in the fiqh apparatus imposes lock-in, and the *mudaraba*'s revocability and dissolution on death actively prevent it. See [[The mudaraba is revocable not locked in]].

The two bindings are in tension, and that tension *is* the trade-off surface. The *qirad* maximizes outcome-coupling and forgoes temporal binding: payoff welded to the realized result, but no duration, no scale, no accumulation. The corporation inverts the settlement — it achieves temporal binding and attenuates outcome-coupling in three specific ways: **limited liability truncates** the downside, **transferability permits exit before realization**, and **diversification dilutes** any single venture's outcome.

So the corporation buys duration and scale by paying in coupling; the *qirad* buys coupling by paying in duration and scale. Stating the trade-off in these two variables replaces a moral intuition with a decomposition, and it maps onto the separable-functions framework directly: capital lock-in and perpetual succession sit on the temporal axis, while limited liability and transferable claims are the coupling-attenuators.
