# Separate the genetic and the methodological claims about economics

Two claims are easily fused and must be held apart. One is **genetic-sociological**: economics is *the semantic reaction to* an organizational shift toward ensemble-average maximization. The other is Peters' **methodological indictment**: economics *remains stuck* in ensemble thinking.

These are logically independent. Economics could be stuck in the expectation operator purely for tractability — expectation is the unique linear, additive, measure-respecting aggregator, and linearity is what makes the machinery solvable — without that stuckness being the ideological reflex of an ensemble-shaped society. Weld the two and an intellectual historian will say you have asserted a base-superstructure story and called co-emergence a causal mechanism. The defensible move keeps them separate and supplies a *mechanism* for the genetic claim, not a correlation.
