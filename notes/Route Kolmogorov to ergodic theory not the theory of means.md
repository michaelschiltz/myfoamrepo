# Route Kolmogorov to ergodic theory not the theory of means

If Kolmogorov is wanted, use him for **ergodic theory**, not the theory of means (where the Kolmogorov–Nagumo quasi-mean actually *supports* the expected-utility patch — see [[Economics had its ensemble moment in 1738 and psychologized it]]). The relevant lineage is Birkhoff–Khinchin–Kolmogorov on when the time average equals the phase average, and under what conditions the equivalence *fails*.

That is the point. Physics did not "move on" by declaring ensemble averages bad — statistical mechanics is built on them. It moved on by developing the **hygiene** to state the ergodic conditions explicitly and to know when they do not hold. Economics' failure is not that it uses ensemble averages; it is that it never developed that hygiene, so it applies the equivalence *unconditionally*. Stated this way, the critique cannot be dismissed as the crude "time good, ensemble bad" reading.
