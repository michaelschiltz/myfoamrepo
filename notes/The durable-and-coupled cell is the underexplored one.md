# The durable-and-coupled cell is the underexplored one

Crossing the two axes yields four cells, and the literature has worked only two of them.

Coupling-high, memory-low: the *qirad* and the single-venture partnership — legible settlement at high frequency, with nothing durable to encode what the settlements revealed.

Coupling-low, memory-high: the joint-stock corporation — archives, routines, standing procedure, and a surrogate evaluation apparatus running on proxies.

The cell carrying the positive claim is the third: **durable forms that did not pay in coupling.** The unlimited-liability partnership bank, the Japanese *ie* and *shinise*, the naties across six centuries — all persisted across generations while leaving the partners' personal estates exposed to the venture's outcome. They bought organizational memory without purchasing it with the liability principle.

This is where the project's own material already sits, and it converts the framework from a critique into a research programme. The question stops being whether the corporation was superior and becomes: **what made the durable-and-coupled cell viable, and what was its ceiling?** These forms plainly did not scale to the Cape route. Candidate constraints to test: kin-based recruitment limits, the correlation of partner estates under a common shock, and the absence of transferable claims as an exit valve.

The fourth cell — low coupling, low memory — needs no investigation. It names failure.
